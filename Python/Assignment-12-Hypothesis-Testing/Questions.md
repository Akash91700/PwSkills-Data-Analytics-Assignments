# Assignment 12 - Hypothesis Testing

---

## Question 1

A company introduces a new customer feedback process.  
The mean satisfaction score before implementing the process was 72.  
After implementing the process, a random sample of 30 customers gave a mean satisfaction score of 78, with a standard deviation of 10.  
At a 5% significance level, test if the new process has improved customer satisfaction.

---

## Question 2

A school claims that their students’ average math score is higher than the national average of 75.  
A random sample of 50 students scored an average of 77 with a standard deviation of 8.  
Perform a one-tailed test at a 0.01 significance level to verify the school’s claim.

---

## Question 3

A retail store wants to check if their new sales strategy increases the average sales from ₹10,000 per day.  
After implementing the strategy for 15 days, the sales data showed an average of ₹11,200 per day with a standard deviation of ₹1,500.  
Conduct a one-sample t-test at a 5% significance level.

---

## Question 4

A factory produces light bulbs with an advertised lifespan of 1200 hours.  
A sample of 40 bulbs is tested, and the mean lifespan is found to be 1180 hours with a standard deviation of 50 hours.  
Test if the advertised lifespan is accurate at a 0.05 significance level.

---

## Question 5

A researcher wants to test if the average height of adult males in a city differs from the national average of 5.8 feet.  
They collected data from 25 individuals and found an average height of 5.7 feet with a standard deviation of 0.3 feet.  
Perform a two-tailed test at a 1% significance level.
